# Code of conduct

Maqam is committed to a respectful, technically rigorous open-source community.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful and specific when discussing code or decisions.
- Critique ideas and implementations, not people.
- Share reproducible evidence and disclose uncertainty.
- Respect privacy, security embargoes, and contributor attribution.
- Help newcomers understand project boundaries and review expectations.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, threats, discrimination, or personal attacks.
- Publishing another person's private information or credentials.
- Deliberately misleading security, benchmark, compatibility, or certification claims.
- Repeated disruption, spam, or promotional posting unrelated to the project.
- Pressuring maintainers or contributors to merge, publish, or disclose before review is complete.

## Enforcement

Report conduct concerns privately to the maintainer through the contact method in [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md). The maintainer may edit or remove content, close discussions, restrict participation, or ban accounts when necessary to protect the community.

Enforcement decisions should be proportionate, documented privately, and focused on restoring a safe working environment.
